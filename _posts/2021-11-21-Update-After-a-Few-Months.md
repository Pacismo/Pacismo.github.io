Hello, everyone.

It has been a bit since I have last posted -- about ten months. Not much has happened between then and now, but what has happened is still pretty significant.

## Significant Changes

First, I have moved back to the U.S., except I now live in Massachusetts. This transition is pretty significant -- not just because of the new lifestyle I have to adapt to, but because I now have to worry about COVID-19. In Brunei -- at least, up until late July -- there were no COVID cases, meaning that nobody had to worry about getting infected by the virus. I didn't have to wear a mask, but I still had to stay in the country, as they wouldn't let you leave without a reason.

Here, you could go anywhere, but you could end up falling ill from a small error -- that's a huge difference. Nonetheless, it's a change I have since managed to acclimate to.

## University

Additionally, I got admitted to the CICS major for UMass Amherst, which has a very good computer science program. So far, it's been easy for me settling in -- could I blame the heavy workloads I had in the IB, or could it be that I didn't enroll in the most strenuous classes available? Either way, I have been doing fairly well starting off, and I wish to keep that up. The following weeks will be relatively light, but I also have to worry about final examinations, which should not be too hard. I am a little behind on some reading, but I can easily catch up during my Thanksgiving break.

## Projects

Additionally, I taught myself Rust! It is very useful for writing console applications and manipulating a thing or two, but that's about it -- there is not a lot that can be done at the moment, which could easily be pinned on my lack of experience using the language. That's okay, though. I will find some things to use it for eventually.

I still seem to struggle with my social skills, but it has not been too terrible (at least, not yet). I have spoken with my R.A. and he had given me some good suggestions and advice for what I could do, moving forward. I am glad that I had this conversation, since I had been worried that I wasn't doing well at all. It fills me with confidence. By just a little.

Nonetheless, I need to start posting here more. Perhaps I could do it weekly? Or maybe once every two weeks? I'll figure out a good rythm eventually. I want to have more to do with what resources I have. Perhaps I'll even re-write some of the utility code here to leverage Rust instead -- that Powershell script isn't looking very pretty.

I also have plans to start making coding tutorials -- maybe I'll start off by showing what I know about C++ in a sensible way, simultaneously teaching the basics of the language and some of the more advanced concepts. I could also try to do the same with Rust, but that would depend on how well I understand the language. This is just for fun, but I am unsure of whether I am able to do this. I have other things on my plate that I need to clean off first, so I'll see where I can make room for that undertaking.

## Closing

It might be a while until my next post -- I don't know if I am willing to make this too casual. I don't know how comfortable I am with posting my feelings on the Internet.

Thanks for reading,  
**-Francisco Santana**
